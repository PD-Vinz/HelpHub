Privacy Policy

DHVSU MIS Department's Issue Reporting and Ticketing Management System

Introduction

The DHVSU Management Information Systems (MIS) Department is committed to protecting your privacy. This privacy policy outlines how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our issue reporting and ticketing management system. By using our system, you consent to the data practices described in this policy.

Information We Collect

We may collect the following types of information:

  • Personal Information: This includes your name, email address, phone number, and any other information you provide when submitting a ticket or report.
  • Usage Data: We collect information about how you use the ticketing system, including your IP address, browser type, pages visited, and time spent on our site.
  • Cookies and Tracking Technologies: We may use cookies and similar tracking technologies to monitor activity on our service and store certain information.

Purpose of Data Collection

We collect data for the following purposes:

  • Issue Management: To effectively manage and resolve reported issues or tickets.
  • User Communication: To notify you about your ticket status, updates, and responses to your inquiries.
  • System Improvement: To analyze usage data for system improvement and optimization in accordance with ISO/IEC 25010 quality standards.

Data Protection and Security

We implement a variety of security measures to ensure the safety of your personal information. Our security practices are aligned with the security and reliability requirements outlined in ISO/IEC 25010. This includes:

  • Access Controls: Only authorized personnel have access to personal information.
  • Data Encryption: Sensitive data is encrypted during transmission.
  • Regular Security Audits: We conduct regular audits to ensure our data protection measures are effective.

Data Retention

We retain your information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, comply with our legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements.

Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or otherwise transfer your personal information to third parties without your consent, except in the following circumstances:

  • Service Providers: We may share information with third-party service providers who assist us in operating our system, provided they agree to keep this information confidential.
  • Legal Compliance: We may disclose your information if required to do so by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities.

User Rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access: You may request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Correction: You may request correction of any inaccurate or incomplete personal information.
  • Deletion: You may request the deletion of your personal information, subject to certain exceptions.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our privacy policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new privacy policy on this page and updating the effective date. You are advised to review this policy periodically for any changes.

Compliance with ISO/IEC 25010

Our commitment to quality software is guided by the ISO/IEC 25010 standard. We strive to ensure:

  • Functional Suitability: Our system meets your needs for reporting and tracking issues.
  • Performance Efficiency: We aim for optimal performance with minimal delays.
  • Usability: Our system is designed to be user-friendly and accessible.
  • Reliability: We work to ensure our system is dependable and available.
  • Security: We prioritize the protection of your data.
  • Maintainability: We commit to maintaining and updating our system regularly.
  • Portability: Our system is designed to function across various platforms.
